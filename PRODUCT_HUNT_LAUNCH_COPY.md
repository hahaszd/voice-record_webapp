### "Why not just use my phone's voice recorder?"
```
Great question! Phone recorders require you to:
1. Unlock phone
2. Open app
3. Click record
4. Start speaking

By then, you've lost 10-15 seconds and potentially your train of thought.

VoiceSpark runs in your browser, already open, always listening. The moment you think of something â†’ just speak. Zero friction.

Plus, VoiceSpark can record system audio (YouTube, podcasts, etc.), which phone recorders can't do.
```

### "Is this safe? What about privacy?"
```
100% safe and private:
- All data stored LOCALLY in your browser
- Nothing uploaded to any server
- No account = no data collection
- You can clear history anytime

Think of it like using a notepad app â€” everything stays on your device.
```

### "How is this different from Otter.ai / Rev / etc?"
```
Great tools, but they focus on long-form transcription (meetings, interviews).

VoiceSpark is designed for quick idea capture:
- Always-on recording (they require you to start/stop)
- No registration needed (they require accounts)
- Optimized for short bursts (30s-5m, not 1-hour meetings)
- System audio capture (record from YouTube, podcasts)

Think: Quick notes vs. full meeting transcripts.
```

### "Will this work on mobile?"
```
The web version works on mobile browsers (microphone only).

I'm working on a native mobile app (Capacitor-based) for better background recording. Coming soon!

Desktop is currently the best experience because you get:
- System audio capture
- Dual recording (mic + system)
- More stable background recording
```
